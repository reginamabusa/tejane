Basotho Music&Dance

The traditional dances and music Basotho played during ceremonies.Some of this dances are danced by both men and women while some are played by either of two genders.

basotho dances

Litobolonya

It is a dance around genre that serves as a rite of
passage,expressing their femininity,grace
and strength.

Mokhibo

A beautiful symobolic dance combining graceful
movements and gestures to tell stories,celebrate
heritage and convey unity.When performing this,
women wear dresses made of a unravelled mealie
meal plastic.

Mokopu

A dance played by women to demostrate culture.

Mohobelo

A trditional dance for men known for its vogorous
movement like strides and kicks, often accompanied by
distinctive humming sound.

Ndlamu

A dance characterized by powerful powerful
foot stomping, high leg lifts and synchronized
movement performedy men in warrior-like attire.

Liphotha

It is characterized by three distinct lines of
performers moving with their own rhythms
and styles.

A dance performed by a large goup of men.

Mokebo

A vibrant dance performed by women
that celebrates feminity ,grace and strength,
ofetn seen at wedding ceremonies.

Mokorotlo

A dance performed by men for the chief during
important occassions,involving rhythmic swinging,
slow foot stamping, and a leader's song with a
group refrain.

Setapa

A dance including foot stamping.

Selialia

A traditional Basotho game and courtship
dance played by young men and women.

Tenye

A dance and courtship dance and social
interaction between young people.

Sephumula

A ceremonial practice associated with the
ritual,which is performed when a girl has
her first menstrual cycle.

Tswana

A dance reinforcing social bonds
celebrating heritage.

Setolotolo

A traditional dance performed by,
women, celebrating important events
like weddings and harvests.

Tholoana

A lively dances showcasing energetic
movements and joy.

Bolo Sela

A dance performed at weddings,
symbolizing love and community
celebration.

instruments

Mamokhorong

A instrument often described as a bow-harp
played holding the resonator against a head or waist,using
a bow made of animal hair to create a unique sound.

Lesiba

A unique wind instrument consisting of a string and
feather,which the player blows on to create sound.

Lekolulo

A flute typically played by herding boys
to imitate the sounds of wild birds, and which can also be
used for vocalisation and in communal music -making.

Setolo-tolo

An extended jaw harp played by men,
ofetn used during entertainment and travels

Thomo

A string instrument played by women,who learn
to play it during their time completing household chores.

Drums

Used in dances like the women's mokhibo,which
where a dancer called terebele may blow a whistle.

whistle

Used as a musical accompaniment in some
forms of traditional music such as litobolonya.

Lekope

A stick pipe made from a hallowed reed,featuring a
plucked string that is made from horsehair or nylon.

Mokhope

A traditional instrument used in Basotho music.

Morupa

A small drum used at girl's initiations,
made from clay a clay pot with a taut skin.

Dikhaere

An instrument used in traditional Basotho music and dance.
